# Analytics Writing and Commentary

Writing on analytics, business interpretation, and how analytical work is changing with AI.

Covers three recurring themes: the foundations that make analytical work credible, the thinking behind tool and workflow choices, and where AI fits into the analyst's job without replacing the judgment that makes analysis useful.

---

## Blog Posts

### [SQL, Python, and Power BI Are Not Competing for the Same Job](./blog-posts/sql-python-powerbi-not-competing-same-job.md)
The tool debate usually starts in the wrong place. Before choosing between SQL, Python, and Power BI, the analyst has to understand what layer of work is actually in front of them — and why the tools are often strongest at different parts of the same workflow.

### [The Part of Dashboard Work People Notice First Is Not the Part That Builds Trust](./blog-posts/dashboard-work-trust-not-visuals.md)
A polished dashboard can look decision-ready while resting on weak logic. Trust is not built at the visual layer — it is built in the analytical foundations underneath.

### [Why Better AI Use Starts With Better Prompting](./blog-posts/better-ai-use-starts-with-better-prompting.md)
Weak AI output is often not a model problem. It is a setup problem. Before spending on agents or automation, it is worth asking whether the task has been defined clearly enough to give the model a fair chance.

## LinkedIn Posts

### [The next useful AI upgrade for analytics may be reliability, not intelligence.](./linkedin-posts/ai-reliability-not-intelligence.md)
Better AI in analytics matters less for sounding smarter and more for becoming a reliable working partner — supporting the analyst when the evidence gets messy and the logic has to be earned.

### [SQL, Python, and Power BI get compared too often as if one of them should win.](./linkedin-posts/sql-python-powerbi-not-competing.md)
Tool choice should come downstream of analytical thinking. The right tool only becomes clear after the work has been understood properly.

### [The biggest AI shift in analytics may not be job replacement. It may be value replacement.](./linkedin-posts/value-replacement-not-job-replacement.md)
AI can carry more of the routine work. The analyst still owns judgment, context, challenge, and the logic behind the decision.

### [Interactive Dashboard Work Is Analytical Thinking Made Visible](./linkedin-posts/powerbi-interactive-dashboard-thinking.md)
The value of an interactive dashboard is not in adding more controls. It is in making the analysis clearer, the signal easier to find, and the next point of focus easier to see.
